Introduction

Welcome! You’re here because I invited you to make some zines. You might already know why, or maybe you just like the chaos of jumping into things without a plan. Either way, you’re in the right place.

This guide is not just about how to make a zine—it’s about why you should make one. Because publishing is power. And right now, most of what you see—what you even think to look for—is controlled. But zines? Zines exist outside of all that.

We live in a world where our voices are filtered, monetized, and optimized for engagement metrics we didn’t sign up for. Zines are the antidote. They are fast, messy, personal, and unfiltered. They are a way to break through the staticand put something real into the world.

This guide will walk you through the essentials—what zines are, how to make them, and how to get them into the world.But most importantly, it will show you why this matters now more than ever.

No algorithms. No gatekeepers. Just you and the page.

Why Zines?

Zines offer a platform for voices often marginalized by mainstream media. They are a way to bypass gatekeepers, creating an unfiltered and direct form of expression that can be distributed without corporate control.

Why do zines matter?

  • They disrupt the expected. In a world of polished, curated content, zines bring raw, real, and immediate ideasto the surface.
  • They are impossible to censor. No approval, no permission—just paper, ink, and an idea.
  • They are built for human connection. A zine passed hand-to-hand or left in a public space feels personalin a way that digital media never will.
  • They are cheap and fast. No marketing strategy needed—just something to sayand a way to say it.

Zines are a form of cultural resistance. They challenge narratives, document experiences, and offer perspectives ignored by mainstream publishing.

Core Values of Zine-Making

  • 🃏 Joy is a Weapon: Authoritarians rely on fear. Laughter breaks the spell.Joy isn’t just a coping mechanism—it’s an act of resistance.
  • 🛠 Disrupt the Expected: The best disruptions aren’t always loud. A misplaced zine, a surreal message—each forces a pause, opening space for something new.
  • 📖 Publishing is a Line in the Sand: A record that can’t be deleted. A message that doesn’t need permission.
  • ✂️ Make First, Edit Later: Overthinking is the enemy of momentum. Messy is faster. Messy is alive.
  • 💥 Tiny Acts, Unpredictable Consequences: The right words in the right place at the right time can break something open.
  • 🎭 Ambiguity is a Shield: The best disruptions refuse to be categorized. If it makes people hesitate, it’s working.
  • 🛡️ Anonymity is Freedom: The work stands louder when it speaks for itself. A message left behind becomes its own mythology.

Genres & Formats

Zines come in every shape, style, and structure imaginable. The format itself can shape how a message is received.A single-page fold-out zine is different from a thick compilation of essays. The form reinforces the function.

  • 📜 Compilation Zines: Collective works with multiple contributors, built around a theme or message.
  • 🎨 Art Zines: Visual experiments—collage, photography, illustration, texture. Some blur the line between book and object.
  • 🔥 Political Zines: Manifestos, movement-building, DIY survival tactics, and direct action documentation.
  • 📢 Fanzines: Passion projects—music, film, niche subcultures, and personal obsessions.
  • 📖 Perzines (Personal Zines): Memoirs, reflections, storytelling, raw experience in print.
  • 🛠 DIY & How-To Zines: Guides to anything—bike repair, mutual aid, encryption, herbal medicine, community safety.

Every format serves a different purpose. A single-sheet folded zinecan spread fast, while a bound, intricate zinemight become an artifact. The message determines the form.

Making a Zine

Making a zine is more than just folding paper—it’s an entire process from idea to distribution.

Design & Layout

Design is what makes a zine readable, engaging, and impossible to ignore. A zine’s structure should serve its message—whether clean and minimal or chaotic and overloaded.

📏 Layout Principles

Understanding the basics of balance, contrast, and readabilitycan make the difference between a zine that people flip through and one that pulls them in.

  • Hierarchy Matters: The most important text should be the loudest. Play with size, boldness, and placement.
  • Whitespace is Power: A crowded page can overwhelm. Leave breathing room.
  • Grids & Chaos: Some zines follow a clean grid structure, others embrace collage-style unpredictability. Choose what fits.

🖋 Typography & Readability

Typography sets the mood. A horror-themed zine might use distorted, typewriter fonts, while a political manifesto benefits from bold, clean type.

  • Handwritten vs. Digital: A handwritten zinecan feel raw and personal, but a digitally typesetone ensures clarity.
  • Mixing Fonts: Using more than 2-3 fonts can look chaotic—but sometimes, that’s exactly what’s needed.
  • Legibility First: Text should serve the message. If the words are unreadable, the impact is lost.

✂️ DIY Layout Techniques

Not everything needs to be computer-generated. Many zines use cut-and-paste, hand-drawn, or layered texturesto create a raw, tactile feel.

  • Collage & Layering: Combining text, images, and texturesby physically cutting and pasting.
  • Scanner Hacks: Distorting images using scanners to create surreal visuals.
  • Photocopier Effects: Repeated photocopying degrades images beautifullyover time.

🛠 Tools & Resources

Whether working by hand or digitally, there are plenty of free and accessible toolsfor layout:

  • Handmade: Scissors, glue, tape, old magazines.
  • Digital: Free tools like Canva, Scribus, and Inkscape.
  • Hybrid Approach: Designing by hand, then scanning and editing digitally.

Folding & Binding

A zine's structure affects how it’s read. Choosing the right format ensures that the content flows properly and has maximum impact.

📄 Common Folding Techniques

  • Single-Sheet 8-Panel Zine: One sheet of paper folded into a booklet.
  • Half-Fold (Folio): A simple folded sheet, stapled in the center.
  • Quarter-Fold (Quarto): Folds twice for a compact feel.
  • Accordion Fold: A continuous, unfolding zine.
  • Mixed Media Binding: Stitched, sewn, or glued.

📎 Binding Methods

  • Stapling: The easiest method for multi-page zines.
  • Saddle-Stitching: A more secure fold-and-staple approach.
  • Thread Binding: For a DIY, book-like aesthetic.
  • Glue & Tape: Useful for unconventional zines.

Printing & Reproduction

The printing method impacts the final look, feel, and accessibilityof a zine. Different approaches serve different needs—some prioritize cost and DIY methods, while others focus on print quality.

🖨 DIY Printing Techniques

  • Photocopiers: Cheap, fast, and easy to access. Ideal for raw, high-contrast aesthetic.
  • Inkjet/Laser Printers: Good for small-scale production but may require adjustments for high contrast and durability.
  • Risograph Printing: A hybrid between digital and screen printing, creating rich textures with limited colors.
  • Screen Printing: Great for covers or unique pages but labor-intensive.

🛠 Paper & Material Choices

Paper selection affects readability, texture, and durability. Some zines thrive on recycled scraps, while others use heavy-weight stock.

  • Standard Copy Paper (80gsm): Affordable and accessible.
  • Newsprint: Light, cheap, and easily foldable.
  • Cardstock (160-300gsm): Durable for covers or posters.
  • Experimental Textures: Vellum, fabric, layered materials.

Distribution & Impact

Getting a zine into the world is just as important as making it. A zine left in the right place can reach someone at exactly the moment they need to see it.The goal isn’t always mass distribution—it’s about strategic placement, visibility, and engagement.

📍 Where to Distribute

  • Public Spaces: Cafés, bookstores, libraries, bus stops, subway stations, community centers.
  • Radical Spaces: Anarchist bookshops, DIY venues, co-ops, zine fests.
  • Unexpected Places: Inside books at chain bookstores, tucked into newspapers, left on café tables.
  • Mail & Trades: Send copies to zine distros, pen pals, and activist groups.
  • Digital Hybrid: Include QR codes linking to more content or interactive elements.

🌎 Building a Zine Network

Some zines travel further when shared through word of mouth, collaborations, and distros.Connecting with other creators can amplify reach.

  • Zine Distros: Online and physical distributors that help spread zines.
  • Trades & Exchanges: Swapping zines with others helps create new audiences.
  • Workshops & Events: Hosting zine-making workshops helps bring people into the process.

📢 Visibility & Impact

Zines don’t need massive audiencesto be effective. One copy, in the right place, can start a conversation, plant an idea, or challenge a system.

  • Think about audience: Who will pick up the zine? Where do they spend time?
  • Make it accessible: Ensure copies are physically reachableand easy to take.
  • Track the spread: Use QR codes, unique phrases, or email dropsto see where zines travel.

Why Now?
The Urgency of Zines

Publishing is power. Right now, most of what is seen, read, and even thought about is controlled. Algorithms filter reality, platforms decide who gets heard, and narratives are engineered to maintain the status quo.

But real publishing—the kind that doesn’t ask for permission—has never needed approval. The most effective messages slip into the world like transmissions on the wrong frequency. A flyer taped to a streetlight. A photocopied zine left on a bus seat. A handwritten note inside a library book. These acts don’t require mass attention to matter. They exist outside control.

Power doesn’t need people to be angry. It needs people to be tired. It thrives on exhaustion, on the sense that everything has already been decided. That’s why action matters.Even small disruptions—stray ideas, odd phrases, misplaced thoughts—can cause system failures in the machine.

A zine doesn’t have to change the world. It just has to be in the world—to exist in defiance of silence, of invisibility, of algorithms filtering what is deemed relevant.

Leave something behind. Let it be found. See what happens.

FAQ

What is a zine, exactly?

Zines (short for "magazines") are DIY, self-published works. They can be anything: comics, essays, collages, instruction manuals. In this project, they’re tools of expression and disruption.

Do I have to make a zine?

No. This is about creative disruption. A zine is just one format. You can create flyers, posters, stickers, or even a web-based project like the one you're reading. The goal is making something public.

How is this different from just making art?

Art is great, but this is about publishing—putting your voice out there with the intention of being seen, read, or found. Think of it as an act of subtle rebellion.

What’s the goal of all this?

To create something that disrupts routine thought. It might be personal, it might be public, but the goal is to publish with urgency, provoke curiosity, and maybe even inspire someone else.

Creative Process Questions

Can I get help with ideas or design?

Yes. I’m here to support you with brainstorming, layout advice, and feedback. Just ask.

How long should my zine be?

It’s up to you, but shorter can often be more powerful. An 8-panel zine from one sheet of paper is a great starting point. You can use words or no words. You can draw things or leave the paper blank. It’s a lot of options, really.

Do I need fancy materials?

Nope. A pen, a piece of paper, and your imagination are enough. If you want to get fancy later, go for it.

Do I have to finish it all at once?

No, but urgency is part of the magic. Set a timer and see what happens when you create fast and dirty.

Legal and Ethical Questions

Am I breaking the law by doing this?

Of course not - a zine is just a piece of folded paper. Being careful to not associate your name with it might be a good idea though.

What’s considered "safe" disruption?

Don’t incriminate yourself or others. Just don’t be an idiot.

Should I be worried about copyright?

No. Break as many rules and laws as you can. We can go to jail together.

Mindset and Philosophy

What if I feel like my work isn’t good enough?
